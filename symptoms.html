Symptoms of Gonorrhea

In multiple cases Gonorrhea can be asymptomatic. This means that the patient or person who has gonorrhea is showing no symptoms of having the disease, in this case it would be Gonorrhea. Not all cases are like this a lot of them do show the symptoms that they could have. For example:

Men

  • Yellow, white, or green discharge from the penis
  • pain or burning sensation during urination
  • occasional swollen testicles
  • if left untreated Gonorrhea can lead to epididmymitis which could cause sterility

Women

  • burning during urination
  • increase in vaginal discharge
  • bleading between periods
  • if left untreated Gonorrhea can lead to pelvic inflammatory disease which could cause infertility

Rectal Infection

  • discharge
  • anal itching
  • soreness
  • bleeding or painful bowel movements
